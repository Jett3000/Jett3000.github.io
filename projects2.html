I also freelance a bit.

This year I made an account on Fiverr to see what it's like in the digital gig economy. It's interesting, to say the least. I quickly abandoned hope of make any degree of serious income on the platform, yet the proposals were so interesting I couldn't fully leave.

You've already seen a bit of my Fiverr work, as the visualization on my homepage is a bit of remixed code from a very cool project. Alexandra Auer asked for an IP-based token generator that would also collect tokens for later gallery style viewing.

I wrote a simple Node.js server and two p5.js sketches, which communicated with each other via socket.io. With a little storage and the App Engine from Google Cloud, we were able to add the project to her site for free!
